Waste management and its impacts on the community

It goes without saying, waste management is on of the most important parts of a healthy city, so why is it that some places are treated better than others? We all pay money to the same council but yet it's clear that some areas face more issues such as flytipping, littering or pollution. Of course, flytipping, littering and pollution doesn't just happen on its own, there's people causing it, but despite this we should all still have a right to live in a clean area void of these issues and the council should give more attention to these troublesome areas if they need more attention instead of letting the issue grow.

Littering, flytipping and pollution isn't just unsightly — it has real impacts on the people living in these areas facing these problems and the level of pride they're able to feel for the place they live. We all deserve to feel proud of the places in which we live and we all deserve to live in a clean area, regardless if there is a small percentage of people causing these issues. EcoEquity aims to make this a reality.

In 2024, Bristol recorded 8,556 incidents of flytipping

How does EcoEquity work?

EcoEquity works by giving the Bristol public a platform to draw attention to the area's of our city that need more action and help by the council. It allows user's of the website the ability to file a report of an incident of flytipping, littering, etc... and then it highlights the areas of our city which need more waste management resources — it does this to draw some resources away from the places which don't face these issues so much and redirects the help where it's needed the most.
